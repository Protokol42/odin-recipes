Description

Fried Rice is the ultimate comfort food and a masterclass in culinary efficiency, traditionally designed to transform leftover "day-old" rice into a savory, texturally exciting meal. The secret to its signature taste lies in the high-heat "wok hei" (breath of the wok), which lightly toasts the grains while they toss with aromatics, crisp vegetables, and scrambled eggs.

This version is a classic vegetable-forward base that is highly customizable. It balances the deep umami of soy sauce and toasted sesame oil with the brightness of fresh green onions. Whether served as a side dish or a standalone main, it's a fast, high-energy dish that rewards quick stirring and a hot pan.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old and chilled)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (divided)
  • 2 large eggs (lightly beaten)
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots (thawed)
  • 1 small white onion (finely diced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger (grated)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 2 stalks green onions (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt (to taste)
  • 1 tsp butter (optional for extra richness)
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Red chili flakes (optional for heat)

Steps

  1. Prep the Rice: If the rice is clumped together, use clean hands or a fork to gently break the cold rice apart into individual grains before you start cooking.
  2. Scramble the Eggs: Heat 1/2 tablespoon of oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Pour in the eggs and scramble quickly until just set. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Wipe the pan clean if needed and add the remaining oil. Add the diced onion and sauté for 2 minutes until translucent. Stir in the garlic and ginger, cooking for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Flash Fry Vegetables: Toss in the peas and carrots. Stir-fry for 1 to 2 minutes until they are heated through and slightly softened.
  5. The Big Toss: Turn the heat to high. Add the rice to the pan. Stir-fry constantly for 3 to 4 minutes, pressing the rice down against the hot surface to toast the grains.
  6. Seasoning: Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, white pepper, and sugar. Toss everything together until the rice is evenly coated and steaming hot.
  7. Final Finish: Fold the scrambled eggs back into the rice along with the green onions (and the optional pat of butter). Toss for one more minute.
  8. Garnish: Taste for salt, then serve immediately topped with sesame seeds.
